Yehuda Prokopowicz - Biography

Yehuda Prokopowicz was born on November 16, 2005. He began learning piano at the age of four with his sister Veronika, a student of Professor Eitan Globerson. His musical education in Kraków led him to the masterclass of Professor Stefan Wojtas, who has been teaching Yehuda since 2018. In 2024, he graduated with distinction from the Władysław Żeleński Secondary School of Music in Kraków. He is currently studying at the Krzysztof Penderecki Academy of Music in Kraków in the class of Dr. Krzysztof Książek.

The pianist is a laureate of over 30 national and international piano competitions, including: 6th Prize at the National F. Chopin Piano Competition in Warsaw; Grand Prix at the I. J. Paderewski Competition in Piotrków Trybunalski; the Chopin Prize and 5th Prize at the "Rubinstein in Memoriam" competition in Bydgoszcz; First Prizes at the EPTA Competition (2022), the A. Harasiewicz Competition (2022), and the Virtual Piano Competition in Malaysia (2020). He has developed under the guidance of outstanding artists and pedagogues such as Prof. Lidia Grychtołówna, Prof. Alexander Kobrin, Prof. Eldar Nebolsin, Prof. Katarzyna Popowa-Zydroń, and Prof. Zbigniew Raubo. In 2022 and 2024, Yehuda had the honor of performing at the 7th and 8th Artur Rubinstein Festivals in Łódź. He has also appeared in prestigious venues such as the National Philharmonic in Warsaw, the Grand Theatre–National Opera in Warsaw, the Kraków Philharmonic, Polish Radio Gdańsk, and Villa Atma. The pianist is an honorary scholarship recipient of the Pro Musica Bona Foundation and, in 2024, he was awarded the "Young Poland" scholarship.

Michał Basista - Biography

He began his musical education at the age of four. In 2017, he graduated with distinction from the First-Level State Music School in Myślenice. In 2023, he completed his studies at the Fryderyk Chopin Secondary Music School in Kraków in the piano class of Dr. hab. Milena Kędra, receiving the highest grade in his principal instrument. He currently continues his studies under her guidance at the Krzysztof Penderecki Academy of Music in Kraków. Since 2012, he has been actively participating in national and international piano competitions. He has also taken part in numerous piano masterclasses and open lessons led by renowned professors and piano virtuosos, including: Andrzej Jasiński, Andrzej Tatarski, Ewa Pobłocka, Piotr Machnik, Alicja Paleta–Bugaj, Konstanze Eickhorst, Barbara Halska, Vadim Rudenko, Viera Nosina, Mikhail Voskresensky, Valentina Igoshina, Magdalena Lisak, Zbigniew Raubo, Piotr Banasik, Szymon Nehring, Takashi Yamamoto, Bradley C. Bolen, Janusz Olejniczak, Angela Hewitt, Nikolai Demidenko, Kevin Kenner, Marc Laforet, Philippe Giusiano, Dina Yoffe, and Sa Chen.

He is a laureate of over 60 national and international competitions. His accolades include the Special Prize at the 2nd Karol Szymanowski International Music Competition (NOSPR, Poland), as well as being a semifinalist of the 2024 Borderland Chopin International Competition (Texas, USA), the 53rd National Fryderyk Chopin Piano Competition (NIFC, Poland), and the 12th International Competition for Young Pianists “Arthur Rubinstein in memoriam” (Poland). He has collaborated with the Polish Sinfonia Iuventus Orchestra and the Lviv Chamber Orchestra. He has received invitations to perform solo recitals at the Royal Łazienki Park in Warsaw, the Steinway & Sons Piano Gallery and the Kawai Showroom (also in Warsaw), at the NOSPR headquarters in Katowice, and at the Royal Castles in both Warsaw and Kraków. He has also appeared in broadcasts on Radio Kraków and TVP3 Kraków. Furthermore, he has had the honor of performing at venues such as the Birthplace of Karol Szymanowski in Zakopane, the Birthplace of Fryderyk Chopin in Żelazowa Wola, the Kraków Philharmonic, and the National Philharmonic in Warsaw. Michał Basista is a scholarship recipient of the Polish Minister of Culture and National Heritage, the Rotary Club Kraków for Artistic Achievements, the Myślenice County Governor in the field of culture, and the Polish Children's Fund. One of his recent major achievements was qualifying for the Preliminary Round and subsequently the First Stage of the 19th International Fryderyk Chopin Piano Competition 2025 in Warsaw.

Krzysztof Wierciński - Biography

He was born on February 22, 2003. He is currently studying at the Fryderyk Chopin University of Music in Warsaw in the piano class of Joanna Ławrynowicz-Just. He has also studied with Leonid Margarius, Arie Vardi, Janusz Olejniczak, Andrzej Jasiński, Elżbieta Stefańska, and Tobias Koch.

He is a laureate of numerous piano competitions, including the 2nd Karol Szymanowski International Music Competition in Katowice (2nd Prize) and the 13th International Chopin Piano Competition in Narva (2nd Prize). He has performed in Europe, North America, and Asia, in venues such as the Krzysztof Penderecki European Centre for Music in Lusławice, the National Philharmonic in Warsaw, the Oskar Kolberg Świętokrzyska Philharmonic in Kielce, and the Orangerie in Parc de Bagatelle in Paris.

Tianyao Lyu - Biography

Born on October 21, 2008, in China. She is currently a student of Katarzyna Popowa- Zydroń at the Ignacy Jan Paderewski Academy of Music in Poznań. She is a laureate of numerous national and international piano competitions, including 1st Prize at the 19th International Piano Competition in Ettlingen (2024) and 1st Prize along with the Grand Prix at the 31st International Fryderyk Chopin Piano Competition for Children and Youth in Szafarnia (2024).

She has performed with orchestras such as the Salzburg Chamber Soloists and the Ningbo Symphony Orchestra. She has given concerts in prestigious venues across Asia, Europe, and the United States, including Carnegie Hall and the National Centre for the Performing Arts in Beijing.

Zuzanna Sejbuk - Biography

Zuzanna Sejbuk is a young-generation pianist who began her piano journey at the age of five under the guidance of Hanna Urbańska in her hometown of Szczecin. Starting in 2019, she studied at the Fryderyk Chopin State Secondary Music School in Kraków in the piano class of Olga Łazarska. She is currently studying at the Fryderyk Chopin University of Music in Warsaw in the piano class of Professor Joanna Ławrynowicz-Just. The young artist actively participates in numerous piano masterclasses with renowned professors such as Dang Thai Son, Hubert Rutkowski, Andrzej Jasiński, Wojciech Świtała, and Ewa Pobłocka. She is a laureate of many national and international competitions, including:

-1st Prize at the Chopin Piano Competition Narva 2020 (Estonia),
-Grand Prix and 1st Prize at Piano Talents for Europe, Dolný Kubín, Slovakia (2021),
-1st Prize at the National Piano Competition in Konin (2022),
-1st Prize at the National Fryderyk Chopin Piano Competition in Turzno (2022),
-3rd Prize at the Jeune Chopin International Piano Competition for Children and Youth, Lugano (2023),
-Grand Prix at the 7th West Pomeranian National Piano Competition in Szczecin (2023),
-Grand Prix at the 3rd “Alternative Competition” of Polish Radio Chopin (2023),
-Grand Prix at the National Piano Competition of the Center for Artistic
Education for Secondary Music School Students in Warsaw (2024),
-1st Prize at the 5th National A. Harasiewicz Piano Competition in Warsaw (2024).

Zuzanna was a finalist of the 53rd National Fryderyk Chopin Piano Competition in Warsaw and has qualified for the First Stage of the 19th International Fryderyk Chopin Piano Competition in 2025. She has performed in prestigious venues such as the Birthplace of Fryderyk Chopin in Żelazowa Wola, the Krzysztof Penderecki European Centre for Music in Lusławice, Villa Atma in Zakopane, the Royal Łazienki Park and the Royal Castle in Warsaw, Holy Trinity Church in Warsaw, the Chamber Philharmonic in Łomża, and the Chopin Centre in Szafarnia. Zuzanna Sejbuk has been a long-time scholarship holder of the Polish Children's Fund in Warsaw and the Pro Musica Bona Foundation in Kraków. She has also received multiple scholarships from the Mayor of Szczecin, twice from the Ministry of Culture and National Heritage, and from the Kraków Club. In 2022, she took part in the Morningside Music Bridge scholarship program for young, talented musicians in Boston, USA.

Jan Widlarz - Biography

Jan Widlarz, born on June 2, 2002, in Warsaw, is currently a student of Professor Katarzyna Popowa-Zydroń and Dr. hab. Paweł Wakarecy at the Feliks Nowowiejski Academy of Music in Bydgoszcz. He is a prizewinner of numerous piano and chamber music competitions, including 2nd Prize at the International Piano Competition in Rome (2016) and 3rd Prize at the International Chamber Music Competition (2018). He was also a finalist of the National Fryderyk Chopin Piano Competition in Warsaw (2022) and received an honorary medal at the 69th Maria Canals Competition in Barcelona.

He has been awarded scholarships from the Polish Minister of Culture and Art as well as the Yamaha Music Europe Foundation. As a soloist, he has performed with the Polish Radio Symphony Orchestra and the Świętokrzyska Philharmonic Orchestra. In Poland, he has appeared at venues such as the Birthplace of Fryderyk Chopin in Żelazowa Wola, the Fryderyk Chopin Museum in Warsaw, and the NOSPR Concert Hall in Katowice. His international concert activity includes performances in Asia (Japan, China), including a recital at the Sapporo Concert Hall. He has participated in masterclasses with distinguished pianists such as Dang Thai Son, Dina Yoffe, Rena Shereshevskaya, Wojciech Świtała, Hinrich Alpers, Xiaohan Wang, Minsoo Sohn, and Michel Béroff.

Jacek Kortus - Biography

Jacek Kortus (born in 1988 in Poznań) graduated with distinction from the I. J. Paderewski Academy of Music in the piano class of Professor Waldemar Andrzejewski. He currently holds the position of Assistant Professor and Head of the Piano Department at the same institution. He is a laureate of many national and international piano competitions, including:

-1st Prize and Grand Prix at the 9th
-International Chopin Piano Competition in Antonin (2005),
-1st Prize at the National Prof. Ludwik Stefański Piano Competition in Płock (2002),
-2nd Prize (1st not awarded) at the 1st National Władysław Kędra Piano Competition in Łódź (2002),
-2nd Prize at the National I. J. Paderewski Piano Competition in Piotrków Trybunalski (2000),
- 2nd Prize at the National Competition in Żagań (2001),
-3rd Prize at the International Competition in Antonin (2000),
-and 3rd Prize at the International Competition in Szafarnia (1999).

In 2003, he was a finalist at the Stefan Seiler International Piano Competition in Kitzingen, and in April 2004, he won a special prize at the 6th International Competition for Young Pianists “Arthur Rubinstein in Memoriam” in Bydgoszcz. In October 2005, he was a distinguished finalist at the 15th International Fryderyk Chopin Piano Competition in Warsaw. Jacek Kortus has performed in leading concert halls in Poland and abroad, including in Germany, France, Belgium, the Netherlands, Spain, San Marino, the United Kingdom, Italy, Romania, Ukraine, Bulgaria, Moldova, Montenegro, Serbia, the United States, Mexico, Brazil, Chile, China, Japan, Taiwan, Israel, Palestine, Morocco, Norway, and Hungary. In 2002, he received a scholarship from the Jolanta and Aleksander Kwaśniewski Foundation "Support for Young Talents." He was twice awarded the scholarship of the Minister of Culture (2002, 2005). In 2006, he was honored with the "Young Art Medal" for outstanding artistic achievements, the Hipolit Cegielski “Young Positivist” Medal for contributions to social work, and was nominated for the "Polityka’s Passport" award. In May 2008, his first solo album was released by "Muza Polskie Nagrania," and in 2009 it was nominated for the prestigious Fryderyk Award. In 2011, he received a scholarship from the Minister of Science and Higher Education for exceptional artistic achievements. In 2015 and 2024, he released an album featuring songs by Polish composers, recorded with the distinguished Polish tenor, Professor Tomasz Zagórski. The second edition was released posthumously after Zagórski’s passing. In 2016 and 2020, he was awarded the Rector's Prize of the Paderewski Academy of Music in Poznań for his contributions to the university. In 2017, he received the honorary distinction "Meritorious for Polish Culture" from the Minister of Culture. In 2022, he recorded a CD featuring the piano concerto by Feliks Nowowiejski with the Poznań Philharmonic under the baton of Łukasz Borowicz. The recording was honored with the Fryderyk Award in 2023.

Jacek Kortus is an Honorary Citizen of the Rokietnica Municipality. In 2023, he was named Personality of the Year in the culture category for Poznań County in a plebiscite organized by Głos Wielkopolski. In November 2023, he defended his doctoral dissertation entitled: “The Symphonization of Sound in the Piano Works of Ludwig van Beethoven, Franz Liszt, and Igor Stravinsky. Reflections Based on Selected Compositions.”

Łukasz Byrdy - Biography

Łukasz Byrdy is one of the most distinctive pianists of the young generation – a laureate of over 20 international piano competitions, performing for more than a decade on the most important stages across Europe, the Americas, and Asia. Born in Katowice, he has won top prizes at the International Fryderyk Chopin Piano Competition in Beijing and the International Piano Competition in Hanover, and has also been awarded in Switzerland, Italy, Malta, and Poland. Following his participation in the 17th International Chopin Piano Competition in Warsaw, his career gained momentum, and his interpretations were acclaimed by audiences from Paris to Beijing. In 2022, he was selected as one of the ten semifinalists of the prestigious Honens International Piano Competition in Calgary (Canada). He is also the recipient of numerous scholarships and artistic awards, including multiple distinctions from the Polish Ministry of Culture and National Heritage and the prestigious “Młoda Polska” (Young Poland) scholarship, one of the highest honors awarded to outstanding young Polish artists.

Byrdy regularly appears at international music festivals, including:
– Chopin and His Europe and the Paderewski International Festival in Warsaw,
– Chopin in the Colors of Autumn Festival in Ostrów Wielkopolski,
– Jerzy Waldorff Festival in Radziejowice,
– Christopher Festival in Vilnius,
– Chopin au Jardin and Chopin à Paris at the Orangery of Bagatelle Park in Paris,
– Aspen Music Festival and School (USA),
– The Polish-Russian Music Festival in Moscow.

He has performed in many of the world’s most prestigious concert halls, including: Harris Concert Hall (Aspen), Bella Concert Hall and Rozsa Centre (Calgary), Orangery of Bagatelle Park (Paris), Sapienza University Hall (Rome), Pushkin Museum and Scriabin Museum (Moscow), as well as concert halls of music conservatories in Beijing and Chengdu, and the Tianfu Center in Chengdu.

In Poland, he has performed with leading philharmonic orchestras and in renowned venues such as:
– NOSPR Concert Hall in Katowice,
– Witold Lutosławski Concert Studio of Polish Radio in Warsaw,
– Krzysztof Penderecki European Centre for Music in Lusławice,
– Mirror Hall of Pszczyna Castle,
– and numerous landmark venues in Warsaw, including the Holy Cross Church, Royal Castle,
Fryderyk Chopin Museum, Chopin’s Birthplace in Żelazowa Wola, Royal Theatre, Palace on
the Isle, and the City Hall Hall of the Palace of Culture and Science.

He graduated from the Universität der Künste in Berlin and the I. J. Paderewski Academy of Music in Poznań, where he now serves as a professor in the Department of Piano. An active pedagogue, Byrdy regularly gives masterclasses both in Poland and abroad – including at the Mount Royal University in Calgary, University of Agder in Kristiansand, the Estonian Academy of Music and Theatre in Tallinn, and the Sichuan Conservatory of Music in Chengdu. He has also been invited on multiple occasions to teach at the prestigious Morningside Music Bridge program at the New England Conservatory of Music in Boston, working with some of the most talented young pianists from around the globe. He has made numerous radio and television recordings, and his performances have been broadcast by Polish Radio, Polish Television (TVP), Lithuanian National Radio and Television, China Central Television (CCTV), and regional stations in Sichuan. In 2017, with the support of the Piotr Kler Foundation, he released his debut album featuring piano concertos by Fryderyk Chopin and Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart.

Maciej Kułakowski - Biography

Born in 1996 in Gdańsk, Maciej began playing the cello at the age of six in the class of Professor Jadwiga Ewald. He is a laureate of the Queen Elisabeth Competition in Brussels and the winner of First Prize and a Special Award at the 10th Witold Lutosławski International Cello Competition in Warsaw. Since 2019, he has been represented by the London-based Young Classical Artists Trust (YCAT). Among the highlights of this season are performances at Wigmore Hall, the Concertgebouw in Amsterdam, Pierre Boulez Saal, the Cologne Philharmonie, Casals Forum in Kronberg, and Schloss Elmau.

As a soloist, Maciej will appear with the Poznań Philharmonic Orchestra, Olsztyn Philharmonic Orchestra, Opole Philharmonic Orchestra, Toruń Symphony Orchestra, and the Baltic Philharmonic Orchestra. In 2022, Delphian Records released his critically acclaimed debut album Beau soir, featuring French music. This was followed by a recording of the complete works for solo cello by Krzysztof Penderecki, released in 2023 by the DUX label. He has performed across Europe and around the world with orchestras such as the National Philharmonic Orchestra in Warsaw, Sinfonietta Cracovia, the Baltic Philharmonic Orchestra, London Mozart Players, Kammerphilharmonie Bremen, Brussels Philharmonic Orchestra, St. Petersburg Symphony Orchestra, and the Calgary Symphony Orchestra.

As a chamber musician, he has appeared at renowned festivals including the Piatigorsky Cello Festival in Los Angeles, Kronberg Festival, Moritzburg Festival, Cello Biënnale Amsterdam, Seiji Ozawa Festival in Japan, Krzysztof Penderecki Festival, Witold Lutosławski Festival, Munich Rising Stars, At the World's Edge in New Zealand, and the Beijing Super Cello Festival. He has collaborated with such artists as Krzysztof Penderecki, Mischa Maisky, Emanuel Ax, Christoph Eschenbach, Viviane Hagner, Giovanni Sollima, Charles Neidich, Arnold Steinhardt, Lawrence Power, and Kian Soltani, as well as conductors including Eivind Gullberg Jensen, Frank Braley, Stéphane Denève, Marek Moś, and Paweł Przytocki, performing a broad repertoire.

Maciej studied with Wolfgang Emanuel Schmidt at the Kronberg Academy and the Hochschule für Musik in Weimar, as well as with Marcin Zdunik at the Stanisław Moniuszko Academy of Music in Gdańsk. He plays a cello by Giovanni Battista Ruggeri from 1700 and a modern instrument by Thorsten Theis from 2014.

Zofia Olesik - Biography

A 19-year-old violinist born in Katowice, she is currently studying at the Ignacy Jan Paderewski Academy of Music in Poznań under the guidance of Professor Marcin Baranowski. She is a laureate of numerous awards, distinctions, and scholarships, including the “Young Poland” scholarship from the Polish Ministry of Culture and National Heritage, as well as scholarships from the Prime Minister of Poland, the Cities of Poznań and Katowice, the Marshal of the Greater Poland Voivodeship, and the prestigious Morningside Music Bridge program in the United States. She is a finalist and prizewinner of over 30 national and international violin competitions, including:

– the Karol Lipiński and Henryk Wieniawski International Violin Competition in Lublin.
– the 7th Jascha Heifetz International Violin Competition in Vilnius.
– the Aram Khachaturian International Violin Competition in Yerevan.
– the Arthur Grumiaux International Violin Competition in Brussels.

She collaborates with the Krzysztof Penderecki European Centre for Music, where she has performed on numerous occasions, including in the “Penderecki Project” with the Vienna Philharmonic and in a solo recital as part of the “Presentations” concert series dedicated to the most outstanding young musicians. She has also worked with the prestigious European Union Youth Orchestra and is part of the Oxford Philharmonic Orchestra’s new program for young artists. Zofia has performed as a soloist with many renowned orchestras, including the Lithuanian National Philharmonic, Sinfonia Iuventus Orchestra, Zabrze Philharmonic, Armenian Symphony Orchestra, Eastgate Youth Orchestra, and the Pomeranian Philharmonic. She also had the honor of performing at Buckingham Palace, representing the new generation of outstanding European artists.

In October 2024 and March 2025, as the only participant from the previous year’s Morningside Music Bridge scholarship program in Boston, she took part in the MMB On Tour concert series in London, performing alongside world-renowned artists such as Maxim Vengerov and Timothy Chooi. She has honed her skills under the mentorship of distinguished musicians such as Noah Bendix-Balgley, William van der Sloot, Midori, Maxim Vengerov, Donald Weilerstein, and Ulf Schneider.
